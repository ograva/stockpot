## 2. Executive Summary

StockPot is a three-sided SaaS platform built exclusively for SMB restaurants in the Philippines. Its north star is simple: **make every restaurant as profitable as its best-run day, every single day.** By tightly coupling purchasing, receiving, preparation, and sales reconciliation into one closed-loop system, StockPot eliminates the operational guesswork that costs restaurant owners margins they can never recover. The product's philosophical foundation — "work the way restaurateurs think" — is not a marketing tagline; it is a hard architectural constraint. Every screen, workflow, and interaction is evaluated first against the question: can a kitchen staff member with dirty hands, in a loud environment, with unreliable internet, complete this task without error in under 60 seconds?

The intellectual core of StockPot is a 30-year-old Excel model from a seasoned restaurant industry veteran, digitized and scaled. This is not a generic inventory system with a restaurant skin. It is a purpose-built system with opinionated data relationships: raw materials have yield percentages, sub-components have prep chains, recipes have par-level targets, and the distance between "what we have" and "what we need" is always one tap away. The platform adds two dimensions the Excel model never could — multi-user real-time collaboration and offline resilience — that transform it from a personal tool into an operational infrastructure.

The primary success hypothesis is this: **if kitchen staff can execute their daily receiving and prep workflows without a keyboard, without internet dependency, and without losing a single data point, operators will see measurable food cost variance reduction within 90 days of adoption.** The platform delivers this through three coordinated applications — the Admin portal (platform operator), the User-App (restaurant operations), and the Vendor Portal (supplier self-management) — backed by Firebase Firestore multi-tenant architecture, Angular 21 signals-based reactive state, and an offline-first `StoreForwardService` sync layer that treats connectivity as optional, not required.

## 3. Target Users & Personas

### Persona 1: The Owner / Franchisor
- **Primary Usage Scenario:** Reviewing daily food cost variance at end of day from a desktop browser. Approving purchase orders generated by the kitchen manager. Configuring new recipes after a menu change. Setting weekly purchasing budgets.
- **Core Job-to-be-Done:** *"When I review last night's performance, I want to immediately see if our actual ingredient consumption matched what our recipes predicted — so I can identify waste or theft before it becomes a trend."*
- **Access Level:** Full access to all user-app routes. Configuration, approvals, reconciliation, and alert management.

### Persona 2: The Kitchen Manager / Head Chef
- **Primary Usage Scenario:** Starting the morning shift, checking what needs to be ordered, generating a PO, dispatching it to the right vendor, then overseeing receiving when goods arrive.
- **Core Job-to-be-Done:** *"When I start my shift, I want to immediately see what's low and get a purchase order drafted and out the door in under 5 minutes — so I can keep cooking instead of managing paperwork."*
- **Access Level:** Kitchen Execution Hub, PO management, stock adjustments. Cannot access master data configuration or reconciliation reports.

### Persona 3: Kitchen Staff
- **Primary Usage Scenario:** Receiving a delivery at the back door on a 10-inch tablet, tapping through the receiving checklist, and getting back to their station — even if the internet is down.
- **Core Job-to-be-Done:** *"When a delivery arrives, I want to tap through what came in, confirm quantities, and have it recorded — without typing anything or being connected to the internet."*
- **Access Level:** Kitchen Home task list, receiving, and prep batching only. Read-only stock views.

### Persona 4: The Vendor / Supplier Representative
- **Primary Usage Scenario:** Logging into the Vendor Portal weekly to update prices on their current product list and marking an item as out of stock when their supply chain is disrupted.
- **Core Job-to-be-Done:** *"When my prices change, I want to update them once in the portal so all my restaurant clients automatically see the new pricing on their next PO — without calling each one individually."*
- **Access Level:** Vendor Portal only. Scoped to their own `vendors/{vendorId}` Firestore document.

## 4. Feature Modules

> **Two-Tier Data Principle (applies to all MSTR stories):** Platform-managed data (ingredients, UoMs, suppliers) is always preferred as it provides automatic pricing and availability updates. User-created custom data is fully supported but requires manual maintenance. This distinction must be visible in the UI at all times.

### MODULE AUTH — User-App Authentication & Onboarding
**Prefix:** `AUTH` | **Sprint:** 1 | **App:** user-app

Authentication is the entry point for all restaurant-side users. It handles three distinct roles — `owner`, `manager`, and `staff` — each with meaningfully different route permissions and UI surface areas. The first-run wizard is architecturally critical: it creates the Firestore tenant document (`restaurants/{restaurantId}`) that scopes every downstream data operation. Auth state is surfaced exclusively via the `CoreService` signal; no component re-queries Firebase Auth directly.

| Story | Title | User Statement | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| AUTH-001 | Login with Email & Password | As a restaurant user, I want to log in with my email and password so that I can access my restaurant's StockPot dashboard. | High |
| AUTH-002 | Role-Based Route Guards | As a platform architect, I want route guards to enforce role-based access so that kitchen staff cannot reach owner-only configuration pages. | High |
| AUTH-003 | First-Run Restaurant Setup Wizard | As a new restaurant owner, I want a guided setup wizard on first login so that my restaurant tenant is created and I can start immediately. | High |
| AUTH-004 | Profile & Password Management | As any authenticated user, I want to update my display name and change my password so that my account stays current. | Med |

**AUTH-001 Acceptance Criteria:**
1. Firebase Auth `signInWithEmailAndPassword` is used; no custom auth backend.
2. Failed login displays an inline error with `data-test-id="auth-login-error"`.
3. Successful login redirects to `/dashboard` based on user role signal from `CoreService`.
4. Session persists across browser refresh via Firebase Auth persistence.
5. Login page has `data-test-id="auth-login-email"` and `data-test-id="auth-login-submit"`.

**AUTH-002 Acceptance Criteria:**
1. `owner` role can access all user-app routes.
2. `manager` role is blocked from `/settings/master-data` and all nested routes.
3. `staff` role is restricted to `/kitchen/*` routes only.
4. Unauthorized route access redirects to `/unauthorized` with `data-test-id="auth-unauthorized-message"`.

**AUTH-003 Acceptance Criteria:**
1. Wizard triggers only when `restaurants/{restaurantId}` document does not yet exist in Firestore.
2. Wizard collects: restaurant name, address, currency (default PHP), and timezone.
3. On completion, Firestore document created at `restaurants/{restaurantId}` using `serialize()` with `SCHEMA_VERSION`.
4. User cannot proceed past each wizard step without completing required fields.
5. Wizard has `data-test-id="auth-setup-wizard"` on the container.

### MODULE MSTR — Restaurant Master Data Setup
**Prefix:** `MSTR` | **Sprint:** 1 | **App:** user-app

Master Data is the backbone of all cost calculations. Without accurate raw material costs, yield percentages, and sub-component definitions, nothing downstream — PO generation, variance reporting, alert thresholds — can be trusted. This module is intentionally owner-facing: desktop-optimized with configuration-dense UIs. The two-tier design principle applies throughout: platform catalog data is preferred; user-created custom data is fully supported. **MSTR-008 (back-calculation engine) is a hard dependency for the entire REPO module and must complete before Sprint 2.**

| Story | Title | User Statement | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| MSTR-001 | Select Active UoMs from Platform Library | As a restaurant owner, I want to choose which platform UoMs are active for my restaurant so that my team only works with relevant measurements. | High |
| MSTR-002 | Raw Material Setup — Platform Catalog | As a restaurant owner, I want to add raw materials by searching the platform's Master Ingredient Catalog so that setup is fast and pricing can be kept current automatically. | High |
| MSTR-003 | Raw Material Setup — Custom Ingredient | As a restaurant owner, I want to add a custom raw material not in the platform catalog so that I can track any ingredient my restaurant uses. | High |
| MSTR-004 | Link Raw Material to Supplier | As a restaurant owner, I want to link each raw material to one or more suppliers so that PO generation knows where to route reorder requests. | High |
| MSTR-005 | Sub-Component CRUD | As a restaurant owner, I want to define sub-components with yield percentages and ingredient chains so that recipe costs account for prep losses accurately. | High |
| MSTR-006 | Recipe CRUD & Portion Definition | As a restaurant owner, I want to create recipes with portion counts and serving sizes so that the system knows what one serving consumes. | High |
| MSTR-007 | Recipe Ingredient Chain Mapping | As a restaurant owner, I want to map recipes to their sub-components and direct raw materials with quantities so that I can see the full theoretical cost per dish. | High |
| MSTR-008 | Par Level Config & Back-Calculation Engine | As a restaurant owner, I want to set minimum portion targets per recipe and have the system back-calculate the raw material minimums so that the Auto-PO engine has thresholds to work from. | High |

**MSTR-008 Acceptance Criteria (critical path):**
1. Per recipe, owner sets `parPortions` (minimum portions to maintain at all times).
2. System traverses the full ingredient chain: `parPortions` → sub-component quantities → raw material minimum stock (in storage UoM), accounting for yield % at each link.
3. Calculated minimums are stored as `rawMaterial.parMinimum` in Firestore and are the trigger threshold consumed by `REPO-001`.
4. Any change to `parPortions`, a recipe's ingredient chain, or a sub-component's yield % automatically triggers a full recalculation for all affected ingredients.
5. Recalculation is initiated by a Cloud Function (not client-side) to ensure consistency across concurrent users.

### MODULE REPO — Smart PO & Replenishment Engine
**Prefix:** `REPO` | **Sprint:** 2 | **App:** user-app
**Hard Dependency:** MSTR-008 must be complete before REPO is implemented.

This module converts the mathematical output of the Master Data module into actionable purchasing decisions. The Auto-PO engine compares current stock levels against `rawMaterial.parMinimum` values, identifies shortfalls, maps them to the correct supplier — preferring platform-connected vendors for live pricing, falling back to manually entered cost for custom suppliers — and generates a draft PO ready for manager review. The target: a PO dispatched in under 5 minutes from shift start.

| Story | Title | User Statement | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| REPO-001 | Shortfall Dashboard | As a kitchen manager, I want to see all raw materials currently below their par minimum in one view so that I can immediately assess what needs to be ordered. | High |
| REPO-002 | Auto-Generate PO from Shortfalls | As a kitchen manager, I want to generate a recommended Purchase Order from the shortfall list in one action so that I can dispatch orders without manually calculating quantities. | High |
| REPO-003 | Manual PO Creation & Editing | As a kitchen manager, I want to create a PO manually and edit its line items so that I can handle ad-hoc or non-par-driven orders. | Med |
| REPO-004 | PO Approval Workflow | As a restaurant owner, I want to review and approve purchase orders before they are dispatched so that I maintain budget control. | High |
| REPO-005 | PO History & Status Tracking | As a kitchen manager, I want to see the full history of all purchase orders and their current status so that I can track which deliveries are pending. | Med |

**REPO-002 Acceptance Criteria:**
1. "Generate PO" creates a draft PO grouping shortfall line items by supplier within 3 seconds for up to 50 ingredients.
2. Quantities are calculated to bring stock from current level to `parMinimum` plus a configurable buffer percentage (default 10%).
3. PO uses platform supplier's live Vendor Portal price if available; falls back to manually entered price otherwise; clearly labels which price source was used.
4. Draft PO is fully editable before submission — manager can adjust quantities, remove lines, or change supplier.
5. `data-test-id="repo-generate-po-button"` on the primary action.

### MODULE SYNC — Offline Sync & Receiving
**Prefix:** `SYNC` | **Sprint:** 2 | **App:** user-app

Offline reliability is non-negotiable. Kitchen internet outages are rated **High likelihood, High impact** in the ProjectBrief. This module wraps the `StoreForwardService` to give kitchen staff a seamless experience regardless of connectivity state. All write operations from the KTCH module pass through SYNC when offline. The module provides a transparent, status-first UI: staff always know what is queued, syncing, or confirmed. Zero data loss is the only acceptable outcome.

| Story | Title | User Statement | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| SYNC-001 | Download Expected Arrivals for Offline | As a kitchen staff member, I want to download today's expected deliveries to my device before going offline so that I can receive goods without internet. | High |
| SYNC-002 | Execute Receiving Workflow Fully Offline | As a kitchen staff member, I want to complete the goods receiving workflow when the internet is unavailable so that deliveries are never delayed by connectivity issues. | High |
| SYNC-003 | Queue Drain & Sync on Reconnect | As a kitchen staff member, I want my offline records to automatically sync to Firebase when my connection is restored so that I don't have to take any manual action. | High |
| SYNC-004 | Offline Status Indicator & Sync Health | As a kitchen manager, I want to see the current sync status of my device at all times so that I'm never unsure whether my data has been saved. | High |

**SYNC-002 Acceptance Criteria:**
1. Receiving dialog functions identically offline, using cached PO data from `StoreForwardService`.
2. Each tap-to-confirm action is recorded in the local forward queue using `serialize()` transform.
3. Offline operations persist across app close and reopen (persisted to IndexedDB or equivalent).
4. A visible offline banner with `data-test-id="sync-offline-banner"` is displayed throughout the offline session.
5. No data written to Firestore during offline mode — all writes queue locally first.

**SYNC-003 Open Question — Conflict Resolution:** What happens when an offline stock adjustment conflicts with an online stock count made by another user during the same period? Last-write-wins or manual review queue? **Watson must define this before SYNC-003 is implemented.** See Open Questions §8.

### MODULE HWBR — Local Hardware Bridge
**Prefix:** `HWBR` | **Sprint:** 2 | **App:** local-bridge (new Node.js Express server)
**⚠️ Watson Dependency:** New `/local-bridge/` folder must be scaffolded in the monorepo. Architecture decision required on deployment model before any HWBR story is implemented.

The Hardware Bridge is a local Node.js Express server running on-site (on a kitchen PC or a low-cost single-board computer) that proxies communication between the StockPot PWA and physical kitchen peripherals — weighing scales and thermal receipt printers. This eliminates cloud round-trips for hardware I/O and ensures peripheral operations work even during server connectivity issues.

| HWBR-004 | Bridge Connectivity Configuration | As a restaurant owner, I want to configure the local bridge server URL in app settings so that the user-app can discover the hardware. | High |

**HWBR-001 Acceptance Criteria:**
1. `/local-bridge/` folder created with its own `package.json`, `tsconfig.json`, and `src/index.ts`.

### MODULE RCNC — Reconciliation & Variance Auditing
**Prefix:** `RCNC` | **Sprint:** 3 | **App:** user-app

Reconciliation closes the loop between what was theoretically consumed — based on POS sales multiplied through the full recipe ingredient chain — and what actually remains in physical inventory. This module ingests daily POS CSV exports, runs the deduction engine through every recipe in the chain, and surfaces variance reports that let the owner immediately identify discrepancies pointing to waste, theft, or recipe drift. This module is the analytical payoff of all the foundational data set up in Sprint 1.

| Story | Title | User Statement | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| RCNC-001 | CSV POS Sales Data Upload & Validation | As a restaurant owner, I want to upload my daily POS export as a CSV so that the system can calculate theoretical ingredient consumption. | High |
| RCNC-002 | Theoretical Deduction Run | As a restaurant owner, I want the system to automatically calculate how much of each raw material was theoretically consumed from today's sales so that I have a baseline for comparison. | High |
| RCNC-003 | Expected vs. Actual Count Sheet | As a restaurant owner, I want to see and export a count sheet comparing expected stock to physical count so that I can identify variances. | High |
| RCNC-004 | Variance Drill-Down | As a restaurant owner, I want to click into a flagged ingredient and see which recipe contributed to the discrepancy so that I can investigate the root cause. | High |
| RCNC-005 | Historical Variance Trend View | As a restaurant owner, I want to see variance trends across the past 30 days by ingredient so that I can identify recurring problem areas. | Med |

**RCNC-002 Acceptance Criteria:**
1. Deduction run traverses: sold recipes → sub-components → raw materials using the full MSTR ingredient chain, applying yield % at each link.
2. Deduction result stored at `restaurants/{restaurantId}/reconciliations/{date}` using `serialize()` with `SCHEMA_VERSION`.
3. Run is idempotent — re-running the same date replaces the previous result cleanly.
4. Unmatched POS items (not mapped to any recipe) are listed in a review screen before deduction is confirmed.
5. Cloud Function executes the deduction; not computed client-side. `data-test-id="rcnc-run-deduction-button"` triggers the function call.

### MODULE ALRT — Alert Engine
**Prefix:** `ALRT` | **Sprint:** 3 | **App:** user-app + Cloud Functions

Alerts convert passive data into proactive operational signals. The Alert Engine monitors raw material stock levels, PO spend vs. budget, and reconciliation variance in near-real-time via Firestore triggers in Cloud Functions. Stockout alerts fire before service is impacted; budget alerts fire before an overspend is locked in. This module directly fulfills the ProjectBrief KPI of 100% proactive intervention coverage.

| Story | Title | User Statement | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ALRT-001 | Stockout Alert Configuration | As a restaurant owner, I want to configure alert thresholds per raw material so that I am notified before any ingredient hits a critical level. | High |
| ALRT-002 | Over-Budget PO Alert | As a restaurant owner, I want to be alerted when a drafted PO total exceeds my configured weekly purchasing budget so that I can review before approving. | High |
| ALRT-003 | In-App Notification Delivery | As a restaurant owner or manager, I want to see alert notifications inside the app so that I don't miss critical events during my shift. | High |
| ALRT-004 | PWA Push Notification Opt-In | As a restaurant owner, I want to opt in to push notifications so that I receive stockout and budget alerts even when the app is not open. | Med |

**ALRT-001 Acceptance Criteria:**
1. Per raw material, owner sets a `criticalThreshold` value in the ingredient's storage UoM.
2. Firestore Cloud Function trigger fires when `currentStock` written at or below `criticalThreshold`.
3. Alert settings stored at `restaurants/{restaurantId}/alertConfig/{ingredientId}`.
4. Owner can enable/disable alerts per ingredient independently of the par minimum setting.
5. Alert fires only once per threshold breach, not repeatedly until stock is replenished.

## 5. User Journey Map — Primary Persona: Kitchen Manager

**Scenario: Morning shift start through end-of-day reconciliation.**

1. **Open App (Login)** — Kitchen Manager opens user-app on tablet, authenticates via email/password. `CoreService` signal resolves the user role as `manager`. App routes to `/dashboard`.

2. **Check Kitchen Home** — Navigates to the Kitchen Execution Hub at `/kitchen`. Task cards show: 3 raw materials below par (amber), 1 pending receiving task (approved PO from yesterday).

3. **Review Shortfall Dashboard** — Opens `/replenishment`. A list of 3 raw materials is below `parMinimum`. Two rows show live pricing from platform-connected suppliers (Vendor Portal badge). One row shows a manually entered price from a custom supplier.

4. **Generate Auto-PO** — Taps "Generate PO." System creates a draft PO in under 3 seconds, grouping 3 line items by 2 suppliers. Manager reviews, adjusts the quantity on one item, removes a line they already have stock for, and submits for owner approval.

5. **Owner Approves PO** — Owner (on desktop browser) receives an in-app notification for a pending PO. Reviews the PO total against the weekly budget — within limit. Approves. PO status → `APPROVED`. Manager receives an in-app confirmation.

6. **Prepare for Offline** — Before heading to the delivery bay (poor signal), manager taps "Prepare for Offline" on Kitchen Home. App downloads the `APPROVED` PO to local cache via `StoreForwardService`. The SYNC status chip in the header turns amber (`OFFLINE`).

7. **Receive Delivery (Fully Offline)** — Delivery arrives. Kitchen staff opens the Receiving dialog from the task card. Steps through each item one at a time. For bulk items, taps "Read Scale" — the hardware bridge pushes the scale weight directly to the quantity field. All confirmations queue locally in `StoreForwardService`.

8. **Reconnect & Sync** — Staff walks back to the main kitchen with stronger signal. SYNC chip turns blue (`SYNCING`). Queue drains against Firestore in FIFO order. Chip turns green (`ONLINE`). "All records saved" confirmation appears. PO transitions to `RECEIVED`.

9. **End of Day — CSV Upload** — Owner uploads today's POS export CSV at `/reconciliation/upload`. System auto-matches 96% of items to recipes. Two items require manual mapping in the review screen. Owner confirms and triggers the deduction run.

10. **Review Count Sheet** — Expected vs. Actual Count Sheet is generated. One ingredient (chili paste) shows in red (>5% variance). Both standard (expected) and manual (actual) counts are visible side by side.

11. **Variance Drill-Down** — Owner taps the chili paste row. Drill-down traces: chili paste → "house chili sauce" sub-component → "Spicy Wings" recipe → 47 portions sold. The theoretical consumption is correct; the physical count is short. Owner logs a waste adjustment and notes it for the morning meeting.

12. **Alert Fires** — The chili paste `currentStock` after deduction crosses the `criticalThreshold`. A Cloud Function fires. Owner receives an in-app notification (bell icon, unread badge increments) and a PWA push notification on their phone. Tomorrow's PO generation will include it automatically.

## 7. Out of Scope (v1)

1. **Direct POS system API integration** — CSV upload is the only POS data ingestion method in v1. Live API integration with specific POS vendors (Square, Lightspeed, etc.) is planned for v3.
2. **Employee payroll, time-tracking, and scheduling** — Permanently out of scope. StockPot is an inventory and recipe management platform, not an HR tool.
3. **ERP / accounting ledger integration** — No automatic journal entries to QuickBooks, Xero, or similar systems. Planned for v4.
4. **Multi-branch / franchise consolidation reporting** — v1 supports one restaurant per tenant account. Cross-branch analytics and consolidated P&L reporting is a v2 feature.
5. **In-app messaging between restaurants and suppliers** — The Vendor Portal allows catalog self-management; real-time chat, negotiation tools, or order confirmation workflows are out of scope.
6. **Complex multi-level PO approval chains** — v1 supports a single-level approval: manager submits, owner approves. Multi-level hierarchies (e.g., franchisee → franchisor → finance) are out of scope.
7. **Vendor self-registration** — In v1, suppliers are invited by the platform admin only. Public supplier self-registration is planned for v2.
8. **Advanced forecasting and demand planning** — AI-driven replenishment suggestions or seasonal par-level adjustments are not in scope for v1.
